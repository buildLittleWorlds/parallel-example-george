## Candidate sources

1. [Enhancing the Readability of Online Patient Education Materials Using Large Language Models: Cross-Sectional Study](https://consensus.app/papers/details/ce1e1b29d03a59cd8c2633c29222f8eb/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   John Will, Mahin Gupta, J. Zaretsky, Aliesha Dowlath, Paul Testa, and Jonah Feldman, 2024, *Journal of Medical Internet Research*, citation count: 13.  
   Relevance: Very close to the project. It evaluates whether LLMs can improve readability while preserving accuracy and understandability.

2. [Large language models and bariatric surgery patient education: a comparative readability analysis of GPT-3.5, GPT-4, Bard, and online institutional resources](https://consensus.app/papers/details/aa7a6d7dafd75ef78c14567c6fae5634/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   N. Srinivasan et al., 2024, *Surgical Endoscopy*, citation count: 35.  
   Relevance: Useful because it compares institutional patient education material with LLM-generated and simplified responses.

3. [The role of large language models in improving the readability of orthopaedic spine patient educational material](https://consensus.app/papers/details/f0fe908932c85d238f9acb85cd102298/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Melissa Romoff, Madison Brunette, Melanie K. Peterson, Sohaib Hashmi, and Michael S. Kim, 2025, *Journal of Orthopaedic Surgery and Research*, citation count: 4.  
   Relevance: Useful for explaining readability metrics and the need to preserve accuracy while simplifying.

4. [Large language models in patient education: a scoping review of applications in medicine](https://consensus.app/papers/details/6e0269f9db5b5ba5a242bcf5516ef4f2/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Serhat Aydin, Mert Karabacak, Victoria Vlachos, and Konstantinos Margetis, 2024, *Frontiers in Medicine*, citation count: 70.  
   Relevance: Broad background on LLMs in patient education, including applications and challenges.

5. [Can AI Improve the Readability of Patient Education Information in Gynecology?](https://consensus.app/papers/details/e85bd38be52e5658aec06e12a061845a/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   N. Daram, Rose A. Maxwell, Josette D'Amato, and Jason C. Massengill, 2025, *American Journal of Obstetrics and Gynecology*, citation count: 1.  
   Relevance: Useful as a cautionary source direction because the abstract reports that LLM simplification may fail to meet recommended reading levels or create an illusion of simplicity.

## How these sources would support the paper

- Sources 1, 2, and 3 support the readability-method angle.
- Source 4 gives the broader patient-education context.
- Source 5 supports the limitation: shorter or simpler-looking text may not actually be accessible enough.

## Citation caution

Do not cite these sources as proof that a classroom health explainer is medically accurate. A safer claim is: "Research suggests LLMs can improve readability, but accuracy, completeness, and human review remain important."
